Fluid Balance Report

Fluid Balance is essential for monitoring the patient's fluid equilibrium. Intake and output data are recorded by the nursing technician throughout their 12-hour shift, continuing the recording from the previous shift, with careful notation in the medical record. The calculation and closing of the Fluid Balance, which covers 24 hours, is the responsibility of the nurse on duty and is fundamental for precise clinical decision-making.

Attention, professionals:

  • Nursing Technician: Remember to record all fluid intake and output values and communicate the values to the nurse at the end of the shift.
  • Nurse: Do not forget to perform the closing of the 24-hour fluid balance to guarantee quality patient care.

This practice aligns directly with Patient Safety Goal 02: Effective communication among the multidisciplinary team. The nursing technician communicates patient fluid intake and output to the nurse at the end of the shift. After closing the fluid balance, the nurse communicates the balance value to the next nurse and the attending physician during shift handover. Effective team communication improves the quality of patient care.

NOTE: IF THE FLUID BALANCE IS POSITIVE, THE PATIENT IS RETAINING FLUIDS. IF THE FLUID BALANCE IS NEGATIVE, THE PATIENT HAS FLUID LOSSES (IF MUCH HIGHER THAN INTAKE). IT IS NECESSARY TO OBSERVE SIGNS OF DEHYDRATION. In both cases, develop a care plan and inform the physician.
