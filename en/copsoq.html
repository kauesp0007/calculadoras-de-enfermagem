Copenhagen Psychosocial Questionnaire II

The Copenhagen Psychosocial Questionnaire II (COPSOQ) is a fundamental instrument for assessing work organization and psychosocial risk management, and is part of the tools to be adopted by the corporate world for implementation and compliance with the update of Regulatory Standard NR-1, which came into effect this year (2026).

About the Questionnaire Application (Long Version)

Format: The Long Version consists of 119 questions (in-depth).

Likert Scale: 5 response options scored from 1 to 5.

Time: The average completion time is 15 to 25 minutes.

Objective: Identify stress, burnout, and leadership quality.

Mathematical Details of the Assessment

1

Likert Scale and Base Score

The official 1 to 5 point scale was applied according to the direct direction indicated by the worker (ranging from "Never"=1 to "Always"=5, according to the local metric).

2

Inversion of Specific Items

Strict inversion correction occurred in the Long Version for items 51 and 54, undergoing mathematically mirrored inversion (e.g., score 5 processed as 1, score 4 processed as 2) due to their opposite bias to the matrix scale.

3

Calculation of Averages

The assessment summed the total raw values of the form (with the aforementioned corrections applied) and divided by the constant of 119 evaluated variables, thus extracting the worker's Global Average.

4

Division into Tertiles (Traffic Light Rule)

The average score was subjected to strict corporate cutoff points:
• Green (Favorable): ≤ 2,33.
• Yellow (Caution): 2.34 to 3.65.
• Red (Risk): ≥ 3,66.
