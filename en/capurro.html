Capurro Method

Gestational Age Estimation

The Capurro method is a technique used in neonatology to estimate the gestational age of newborns, especially when the date of the last menstrual period (LMP) or an early ultrasound is not available. The examination is performed after birth and evaluates physical and neurological signs of the infant, assigning scores that allow estimating gestational age in weeks. It is applicable for newborns of at least approximately 29 weeks of gestation.

Year of Creation: 1978

Authors: Dr. Haroldo Capurro Alzola and his team.

Area of Use: Delivery rooms and neonatal intensive care units (NICU).

Criteria Evaluated: Skin texture, plantar creases, nipple formation, breast bud size, ear shape (pinna), and scarf sign.

Who Uses It?: Neonatologists, pediatricians, and neonatal nurses.
