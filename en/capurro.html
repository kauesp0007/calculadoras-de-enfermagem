Capurro Method

Gestational Age Estimation

The Capurro method is a technique used in neonatology for estimating gestational age in newborns, especially when the date of last menstrual period (LMP) or early ultrasound is not available. The examination is performed after birth and evaluates physical and neurological signs of the baby, assigning scores that allow estimating gestational age in weeks. It is applicable for newborns with at least approximately 29 weeks of gestation.

Key Information

  • Year of Creation: 1978
  • Authors: Dr. Haroldo Capurro Alzola and team
  • Clinical Setting: Delivery rooms and Neonatal Intensive Care Units (NICU)
  • Assessed Criteria: Skin texture, plantar creases, nipple formation, breast tissue size, ear shape (pinna), and scarf sign
  • Users: Neonatologists, pediatricians, and neonatal nurses

Assessment Criteria Overview

Physical Assessment

  • • Skin texture evaluation
  • • Plantar crease pattern
  • • Nipple formation
  • • Breast tissue development

Neurological Assessment

  • • Ear shape and cartilage
  • • Scarf sign evaluation
  • • Muscle tone assessment

Clinical Application

  • • 29+ weeks gestation
  • • Postnatal assessment
  • • 5-10 minute examination
  • • No special equipment needed
