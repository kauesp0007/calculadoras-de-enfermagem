Apgar Score

Evaluates the physical condition of the newborn (NB) immediately after delivery.

The Apgar Score assesses the physical condition of the newborn (NB) at two crucial moments: 1 and 5 minutes after birth. It evaluates five criteria: heart rate, respiratory effort, muscle tone, reflex irritability, and skin color.

Year Created: 1952

Authors: Dr. Virginia Apgar

Area Employed: Delivery rooms and Obstetric centers

Criteria Evaluated: Heart Rate, Respiratory Effort, Muscle Tone, Reflex Irritability, and Skin Color

Who uses it?: Pediatricians, neonatologists, and obstetric nurses
