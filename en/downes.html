Wood-Downes Scale

Assessment of Respiratory Distress in Newborns

The Downes Scale, or Wood-Downes Scale, is a tool used to assess respiratory distress in newborns, especially those with acute viral bronchiolitis. It helps quantify the severity of respiratory insufficiency, guiding clinical management and the need for interventions such as tracheal intubation.
