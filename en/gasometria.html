ABG Interpretation

Parameters and Normal Values (Adults):

pH: 7.35 - 7.45

PCO2 (Partial Pressure of CO2): 35 - 45 mmHg (Represents respiratory component, acts as acid).

HCO3- (Bicarbonate): 22 - 26 mEq/L (Represents metabolic component, acts as base).

PaO2 (Partial Pressure of O2): 80 - 100 mmHg (Indicates oxygenation).

Steps for Interpretation:

Analyze the pH:
pH < 7.35: Acidosis (acidic blood).
pH > 7.45: Alkalosis (alkaline blood).
pH 7.35-7.45: Normal.

Identify Primary Disorder (Respiratory or Metabolic):
If pH is acidic (acidosis): Check PCO2 and HCO3-.
PCO2 ↑ (above 45): Respiratory Acidosis (Lung problem, CO2 (acid) increased).
HCO3- ↓ (below 22): Metabolic Acidosis (Renal/metabolic problem, lack of base).

If pH is alkaline (alkalosis): Check PCO2 and HCO3-.
PCO2 ↓ (below 35): Respiratory Alkalosis (Hyperventilation, CO2 (acid) decreased).
HCO3- ↑ (above 26): Metabolic Alkalosis (Renal/metabolic problem, excess base).

Check Compensation:

The body tries to correct the primary disorder.
Full Compensation: pH returns to normal, but PCO2/HCO3- are abnormal.
Partial Compensation: pH and PCO2/HCO3- are abnormal, but moving in the same direction to compensate (e.g., in metabolic acidosis, PCO2 is low to correct).
No Compensation: pH and PCO2/HCO3- abnormal, but the compensatory parameter is normal, indicating an acute primary problem.

Analyze Oxygenation (PaO2):

Values < 80 mmHg indicate hypoxemia (low O2 in tissues).

Example: pH 7.20 (acidic), PCO2 60 (high, acidic), HCO3- 24 (normal).
