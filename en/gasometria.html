Arterial Blood Gas Interpretation

Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) analysis is a fundamental test for evaluating critically ill patients. Correct interpretation allows for the diagnosis of acid-base disorders (metabolic or respiratory) and oxygenation status, aiding in therapeutic decisions and monitoring treatment response.

Radial Artery Puncture Technique for ABG:
  • 1- Explain the procedure to the patient;
  • 2- Choose the puncture site;
  • 3- Hyperextend the patient's wrist;
  • 4- Clean the skin with alcohol or another antiseptic solution (Ex: chlorhexidine);
  • 5- Use heparinized syringes (Preference for specific pre-heparinized ABG syringe);
  • 6- Insert a 30x7 needle with the bevel facing against the flow at a 90º angle to the skin;
  • 7- Collect between 2-3 mL;
  • 8- Compress the puncture site for 5 minutes;
