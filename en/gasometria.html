Interpretation Guide

1. Reference Values (Adults):

pH: 7.35 ‚Äì 7.45

pCO2: 35 ‚Äì 45 mmHg

pO2: 80 ‚Äì 100 mmHg

HCO3: 22 ‚Äì 26 mEq/L

BE: -3 to +3 mmol/L

O2 Saturation: 95% ‚Äì 99%

Lactate: < 2.0 mmol/L

2. Step-by-Step Simplified:

A: Assess pH
< 7.35: Acidosis | > 7.45: Alkalosis

B: Primary Disorder
Respiratory (pCO2): pH and pCO2 are opposite.
Metabolic (HCO3/BE): pH and HCO3 are in the same direction.

C: Compensation
The body changes the opposite component to correct pH.

D: Oxygenation
pO2 < 80 mmHg indicates Hypoxemia.

E: Co-oximetry
Assess Carboxyhemoglobin and Methemoglobin.

F: Electrolytes/Lactate
Lactate > 2.0 indicates tissue hypoxia/sepsis.
