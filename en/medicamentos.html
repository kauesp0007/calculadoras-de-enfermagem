Calculation Rule

Preparing medication with a defined concentration - Example

Medical Order: 150 mg of medication.

Available: Bottle containing 300 mg in 2 fl oz (300mg/2fl oz).

To solve this, place what you know (Available) on the top line and what you want (Order) on the bottom line. Remember: same units must be aligned vertically.

300mg --- 2 fl oz

150mg --- x

x . 300 = 150 . 2

x = 300 / 300

x = 1 fl oz

Answer: You should draw up 1 fl oz of this solution to administer 150 mg.

Attention:

In case of doubt, consult the nurse in charge and perform a double-check. Never prepare or administer medications if you are unsure. It is both a right and a duty of the nursing professional to ask for help.

Nurses, it is your responsibility to assess your team's knowledge regarding medication preparation and administration. Conduct constant health education. PREVENT MEDICATION ERRORS AND ADVERSE EVENTS.

Aligned with International Patient Safety Goal No. 3.
