About the APACHE II Score

The APACHE II (Acute Physiology and Chronic Health Evaluation II) is a scoring system used to assess disease severity in critically ill patients, particularly those admitted to intensive care units (ICUs). It combines acute physiological data, age, and chronic health history to provide an estimate of hospital mortality risk. The scale is applied within the first 24 hours of patient admission.

  • Year Created: 1985
  • Authors: Knaus WA, Draper EA, Wagner DP, Zimmerman JE
  • Clinical Setting: Intensive Care Unit (ICU)
  • Primary Users: Intensive care physicians and nurses

Evaluation Components

The APACHE II score evaluates three key components to determine disease severity:

  1. 12 physiological parameters (acute physiology score)
  2. Age points
  3. Chronic health conditions

Risk Interpretation

  • Score 0-9: Low risk of hospital mortality (<10%)
  • Score 10-19: Moderate risk of hospital mortality (10-30%)

Assessment Timing

Assessment should be performed within the first 24 hours of ICU admission using the worst physiological values obtained during this period. This timing ensures accurate baseline severity assessment for outcome prediction and clinical planning.

Scientific References

Primary Reference:

Knaus, W. A., Draper, E. A., Wagner, D. P., & Zimmerman, J. E. (1985). APACHE II: a severity of disease classification system. Critical Care Medicine, 13(10), 818-829.
