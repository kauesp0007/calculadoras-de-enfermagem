Fugulin Scale - Patient Complexity Classification (PCC)

Patient Complexity Classification

Select an option for each area of care according to the Fugulin Scale and calculate the total score for patient classification (PCC).

Year of Creation: 1994

Authors: Prof. Dra. Fernanda Maria Togeiro Fugulin (and collaborators), from the School of Nursing at the University of São Paulo (EEUSP).

Area of use: Inpatient units and ICU.

Criteria evaluated: Mental state, oxygenation, vital signs, motility, ambulation, feeding, body care, elimination, therapeutics, dressing change time, dressing, and skin/mucosal integrity/tissue impairment.

Who uses it?: Nurses.

The Fugulin Scale classifies the complexity of nursing care required for each patient. The higher the score, the greater the complexity and need for specialized care.

Reference:

Fugulin, F. M. T., Gaidzinski, R. R., & Kurcgant, P. (2005). Patient classification system: identification of the care profile of patients at the inpatient units of the HU-USP. *Revista Latino-Americana de Enfermagem*, 13(1), 72-78.
